#100Days of Code

After my coding hiatus I needed some external motivation to recommence my coding practice. I used #100daysofcode as a vehicle to re-aquaint myself with old skills and learn new ones.

The premise is simple: code for an hour a day, very day for 100 days and log your progress.

For me, this was a very humbling experience; but one that gave me permission to reset without expections. The increments were small (I often had no more than an hour to spare) but the overall improvement was significant.

A Vision for Our Sass

January 20, 2015

A List Apart

Sass is a powerful tool in helping us wrangle complex stylesheets. Yet it has its headaches—including troublesome nesting of CSS selectors, code duplication, and tight coupling—that result in messy outputted CSS. Universal standards aren’t a viable answer, as the Sass spec continues to quickly change and grow. Felicity Evans holds that the problem isn’t Sass itself, but the way we use it. In this article, she champions a set of tenets that offers guidelines on how to work with Sass and evaluate new features and techniques.

Email Is (Still) Important And Here Is Why

July 25, 2011

Smashing Magazine

Social media is more than a buzzword. It’s now a lifestyle decision for a lot of companies. Many individuals and organizations have abandoned a traditional web presence (which used to mean a website and email address) in favor of a Facebook page coupled with a Twitter account. However the old methods are often the best. Here Felicity explores why email still has such an important role to play.

Falling for HTML5: Finding Love in the Little Things

January 21, 2011

Smashing Magazine

I’ve lost count of the number of posts that have been written about the big features of HTML5: amongst the most anticipated being rich media (video, audio, canvas) and JavaScript APIs. However, call me a woman of simple tastes, but this is not the sort of thing that gets me swooning. What does? The small additions to the spec that will make the world of difference to the way I code day-in, day-out. This is the stuff fairy tales are made of.
